Basic Search

To enter a query into Greple, just type in a few descriptive words and hit the 'enter' key (or click on the Greple Search button) for your list of relevant results.

Greple performs a literal text search across the pages it has indexed and returns the pages whose content best matches your query.

Multi-word Searches

When you enter more than one word, Greple looks for pages where those words appear next to each other, in the order you typed them. Words may be separated by any combination of whitespace or punctuation in the page, but no other words are allowed between them.

See your search terms in context

Each Greple search result contains a short excerpt from the matching page, which shows how your search terms are used in context on that page. This lets you tell at a glance whether the result is a page you want to visit.

Stemming and Wildcards

To provide the most predictable results, Greple does not use "stemming" or support "wildcard" searches. In other words, Greple searches for exactly the words that you enter in the search box. Searching for "grepl" or "grepl*" will not yield "grepler" or "greplin." If in doubt, try both forms: "airline" and "airlines," for instance.

Does capitalization matter?

Greple searches are not case sensitive. All letters, regardless of how you type them, will be understood as lower case. For example, searches for "george washington", "George Washington", and "gEoRgE wAsHiNgToN" will all return the same results.

I'm Feeling Lucky

Next to the Greple Search button you'll find an "I'm Feeling Lucky" button. When you click it, Greple takes you straight to the top-ranked result for your query, skipping the list of results entirely. It's the fastest way to reach a page when you're confident your query identifies it uniquely.

If your query returns no results, the "I'm Feeling Lucky" button behaves the same as Greple Search and shows the (empty) results page instead.
